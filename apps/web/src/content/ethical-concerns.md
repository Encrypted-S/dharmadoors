# Choosing a Safe Sangha

SanghaMap aims to help you find a Buddhist community where you can practice safely. While the vast majority of Buddhist centers are led by sincere, ethical teachers, there have been documented cases of harm in some organizations.

## Our Approach

We believe in **transparency, not censorship**:

- **All centers are listed** - we don't exclude anyone
- **Centers with documented concerns** are hidden by default
- **You can opt-in** to see these centers if you choose
- **We link to sources** so you can research and decide for yourself

## What We Mean by "Documented Concerns"

We flag organizations only when there is:

1. **Published journalism** from reputable outlets (NYT, Guardian, Lion's Roar)
2. **Independent investigations** commissioned by the organizations themselves
3. **Legal findings** (court cases, settlements)
4. **Open letters** signed by senior students or former members

We do **not** flag based on:

- Anonymous allegations
- Social media rumors
- Theological disagreements
- Personal conflicts

## Currently Flagged Organizations

The following organizations have documented ethical concerns. This does not mean all their centers are unsafe, or that you shouldn't practice with them - many wonderful practitioners are part of these communities. It means you should be informed before joining.

### Shambhala International

**Concern:** Sexual misconduct by Sakyong Mipham Rinpoche

**What happened:** In 2018-2019, Buddhist Project Sunshine released reports documenting allegations of sexual assault and misconduct by the leader. Multiple women came forward with accounts spanning decades.

**Current status:** Sakyong stepped back from teaching. The organization has undergone leadership changes and implemented safeguarding policies.

**Sources:**
- [Buddhist Project Sunshine Phase 3 Report](https://www.lionsroar.com/buddhist-project-sunshine-releases-phase-3-report/) - Lion's Roar
- [Sexual Misconduct Allegations Against Shambhala Leader](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/07/11/nyregion/shambhala-sexual-misconduct.html) - New York Times

### Rigpa

**Concern:** Physical and sexual abuse by Sogyal Rinpoche

**What happened:** In 2017, eight senior students published an open letter detailing physical abuse, sexual exploitation, and financial impropriety. An independent investigation in 2018 substantiated these claims.

**Current status:** Sogyal Rinpoche resigned and died in 2019. The organization has implemented reforms.

**Sources:**
- [Buddhist teacher accused of abuse](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2017/jul/21/buddhist-teacher-sogyal-rinpoche-accused-of-abuse) - The Guardian
- [Rigpa investigation findings](https://www.lionsroar.com/rigpa-sangha-releases-findings-of-investigation/) - Lion's Roar

---

### New Kadampa Tradition (NKT)

**Concern:** Cult-like practices, suppression of dissent

**What happened:** Former members and scholars have raised concerns about high-pressure recruitment, discouragement of questioning, financial demands, and the Shugden controversy.

**Current status:** The organization disputes these characterizations. The concerns are ongoing and debated.

**Sources:**
- [New Kadampa Tradition accusations](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/belief/2012/jul/16/new-kadampa-tradition-accusations) - The Guardian
- [NKT Controversy Info](https://newkadampatradition-controversy.info/)

### Diamond Way Buddhism

**Concern:** Controversial leadership, personality cult dynamics

**What happened:** Concerns have been raised about Ole Nydahl's political statements, his self-presentation as a realized teacher, and deviation from traditional Karma Kagyu teachings.

**Current status:** Ongoing concerns. The organization maintains strong student loyalty.

**Sources:**
- [Ole Nydahl controversy](https://www.lionsroar.com/ole-nydahl-the-karma-kagyu-and-the-controversy/) - Lion's Roar

### Triratna (formerly FWBO)

**Concern:** Historical sexual exploitation by founder

**What happened:** Sangharakshita (Dennis Lingwood), who died in 2018, was accused of sexually exploiting male students over decades. The organization commissioned an investigation and acknowledged harm.

**Current status:** The organization has publicly acknowledged past harm, apologized to survivors, and implemented safeguarding policies.

**Sources:**
- [Triratna founder abuse allegations](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2019/sep/21/sangharakshita-triratna-buddhist-founder-abuse-allegations) - The Guardian

## Red Flags to Watch For

Regardless of affiliation, here are warning signs to be aware of when evaluating any spiritual community:

### Teacher Behavior
- Claims of special powers or enlightenment that cannot be questioned
- Sexual relationships with students
- Financial exploitation or pressure for donations
- Isolation from family or other communities
- Discouraging questions or critical thinking

### Organizational Culture
- "Us vs. them" mentality
- Secrecy around practices or finances
- Rapid escalation of commitment expected
- Shaming or punishment for doubt
- Claims that leaving will cause spiritual harm

### Healthy Signs
- Transparency about finances and governance
- Clear ethical guidelines and accountability structures
- Encouragement of questions and healthy skepticism
- Respect for boundaries and consent
- Connection with broader Buddhist community
